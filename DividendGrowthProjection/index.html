Understanding Dividend Metrics

Dividend Yield

Dividend yield represents the annual dividend payment as a percentage of the stock price. While higher yields can be attractive, extremely high yields may indicate financial distress or unsustainable dividend policies.

Dividend Growth Rate

The dividend growth rate measures how much a company increases its dividend payments annually. Sustainable growth rates typically range from 3-10% annually, depending on the company's industry and maturity.

Payout Ratio

The payout ratio shows what percentage of earnings is paid out as dividends. Lower payout ratios (30-60%) generally indicate more sustainable dividends with room for growth.
