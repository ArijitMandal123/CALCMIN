Time Zone Coordinator Calculator FAQ - Global Meeting Scheduling Questions 2025

What's the best time for global team meetings across time zones?

There's no universal best time. Optimal scheduling depends on team locations, priorities, and meeting frequency. For US-Europe teams: 8-10 AM EST works well. For US-Asia: early morning or late evening rotations. Our time zone coordinator calculator finds the fairest compromise for your specific team configuration.

How do you coordinate meetings across 12+ time zones?

For extreme spans (like US West Coast to Asia Pacific), consider splitting into regional meetings with representatives, use asynchronous communication with periodic all-hands meetings on rotation, or implement follow-the-sun meeting schedules. Our calculator helps identify the least disruptive options.

Should global meeting times rotate regularly?

Yes, rotation prevents timezone bias and ensures fairness. Weekly or bi-weekly rotation works well for regular meetings (alternating between US-friendly and Asia-friendly times), while one-off meetings can use optimal times. Track cumulative inconvenience to maintain equity.

How does daylight saving time affect global meeting coordination?

DST creates 2-week misalignment periods when regions transition at different dates. US springs forward in March, Europe in late March, creating temporary 1-hour shifts. Our calculator accounts for DST transitions and provides adjusted recommendations during transition periods.
