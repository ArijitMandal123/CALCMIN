Course Pricing Psychology & Mathematical Formulas

Core Pricing Calculation Formulas:

  • Value-Based Pricing: Price = Perceived Value × Willingness to Pay
  • Cost-Plus Pricing: Price = Production Cost × (1 + Desired Margin)
  • Competitive Pricing: Price = Competitor Average × Differentiation Factor
  • Price Elasticity: % Change in Demand / % Change in Price
  • Revenue Optimization: Revenue = Price × Quantity × Conversion Rate

Content Value Assessment Framework

Systematic approach to evaluating course content worth and pricing justification:

  • Content Depth: 1.0x basic, 1.5x comprehensive, 2.0x masterclass
  • Production Quality: 1.0x basic, 1.3x professional, 1.6x premium

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I price my first online course?

Start with competitive research, then use value-based pricing. For new instructors, consider penetration pricing (20-30% below competitors) to build reviews and reputation.

Should I price high or low for maximum revenue?

Test both approaches. High prices work for specialized, high-value content with expert instructors. Low prices work for broad appeal topics and new instructors building audience.

How often should I adjust course prices?

Review pricing quarterly. Increase prices as you gain authority and positive reviews. Use seasonal promotions and limited-time offers to test price sensitivity.
