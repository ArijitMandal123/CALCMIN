  • B1 (Intermediate): 350-400 hours - Main points of clear standard input

Language Learning Time Calculator FAQ - Fluency Timeline Questions 2025

How accurate are language learning time estimates?

Our language learning time calculator achieves 80-90% accuracy based on FSI data and linguistic research. Individual results vary based on personal factors, study consistency, and learning method effectiveness. Spanish and French estimates are most accurate due to extensive research data.

How long does it take to learn Spanish vs Chinese?

Spanish requires 600-750 hours to reach B2 fluency (18-24 months with 1 hour/day), while Chinese needs 2200+ hours (5-7 years with 1 hour/day). Spanish is FSI Category I (easiest), Chinese is Category IV (hardest) for English speakers.

What's the fastest way to learn a language in 2025?

Immersion programs combined with structured study are fastest (1.5x normal speed). AI-powered apps, VR environments, daily practice, native speaker interaction, and spaced repetition maximize efficiency. 3+ hours daily can achieve B2 in 6-12 months for easy languages.

Can adults learn languages as fast as children?

Adults have advantages in grammar understanding, study discipline, and analytical skills. While pronunciation may be harder after age 12, adults can achieve fluency faster through focused study methods, reaching B2 level in 1-3 years vs 5-10 years for children.

What are CEFR levels and how long does each take?

CEFR levels: A1 (80-120 hours), A2 (180-200 hours), B1 (350-400 hours), B2 (500-600 hours), C1 (700-800 hours), C2 (1000+ hours). Most learners target B2 for professional fluency, requiring 12-24 months of consistent study.

Which languages are easiest and hardest to learn?

Easiest (Category I): Spanish, French, Italian, Portuguese (600-750 hours). Moderate (Category II): German, Indonesian (900 hours). Hard (Category III): Russian, Hebrew (1100 hours). Hardest (Category IV): Chinese, Japanese, Arabic, Korean (2200+ hours).
